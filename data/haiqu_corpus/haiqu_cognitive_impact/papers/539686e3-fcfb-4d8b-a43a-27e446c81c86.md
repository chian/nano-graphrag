Unravelling the combination of medical and elderly care in China: a comprehensive policy analysis


[Download PDF](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3.pdf)

[Download PDF](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3.pdf)

## Abstract

This study aims to conduct a systematic and comprehensive analysis of the combination of medical and elderly care policy (CMECP) texts and to identify the composition, characteristics, and shortcomings of existing policies. On this basis, optimization suggestions are proposed to improve the policy system for the CMECP, thereby more effectively addressing the health and care needs of elderly individuals. To understand and analyse in depth the current situation and problems of the CMECP, this study first conducts text mining. By using the ROST CM6 tool to analyse the policy focus of CMECP text, this research comprehensively reveals the core issues and main concerns of the policy text. Simultaneously, this study conducts a dynamic topic evolution of policies related to the CMECP based on the LDA topic model and visualizes it using a Sankey diagram. Second, our research employs the PMC index model to quantitatively assess the comprehensiveness and implementation effect of policies. Finally, this study systematically evaluates and analyses the CMECP by integrating text mining and the PMC index model to more accurately understand and assess the developmental direction, implementation effects, and existing problems of China’s CMECP. The results reveal that the average PMC index of the 12 policy samples stands at 6.67, representing an impressively high standard of policy text quality. The study also finds that while China’s CMECP has achieved results in terms of fostering service integration and technological innovation, long-term planning and voluntary policy tools are not being used effectively. In addition, through policy analysis, it can be inferred that many of the policies mentioned emphasize the expansion of telemedicine and the integration of traditional Chinese and Western medicine, but the specific incentive measures and methods need to be strengthened. At the same time, the combination of medical and elderly care services is moving towards home-based and community-based elderly care, but resource allocation and policy coherence need to be improved. This study not only provides valuable insights for improving China’s CMECP but also further promotes the implementation of the Healthy China 2030 strategy.

## Introduction

The ageing of the global population is becoming an increasingly serious global issue. Relevant studies show that the ageing process is accelerating, as Padeiro et al. ( [2023](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR46 "Padeiro M, Santana P, Grant M (2023) Global aging and health determinants in a changing world. In: Aging. Academic Press, pp 3–30")) noted that the world’s population aged 65 and above will double from 2019 to 1.5 billion, accounting for 16.0% of the total population by 2050. Over the next 20–30 years, the impact of ageing will further expand (Sander et al. [2015](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR51 "Sander M, Oxlund B, Jespersen A et al. (2015) The challenges of human population ageing. Age Ageing 44(2):185–187.                    https://doi.org/10.1093/ageing/afu189                                    ")), and the challenges will become more severe. According to survey data related to the global ageing society (Li [2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR36 "Li XY (2024) Discussion on the issues of population aging and the development of the pension system in China. Mark Manag Rev 10:106–108.                    https://doi.org/10.19921/j.cnki.1009-2994.2024-10-0106-036                                    ")), China is at the forefront of countries with a serious risk of population ageing worldwide. As reported by the most recent data provided by China’s National Bureau of Statistics in 2024, as of 2023, the population aged 60 years and above accounted for 21.1% of the entire population in China, while the population aged 65 and above accounts for 15.4% of the total population. Moreover, according to the latest statistics, China is expected to move from the current moderately ageing society to a severely ageing society around 2035 (Sang et al. [2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR52 "Sang M, Jiang J, Huang X et al. (2024) Spatial and temporal changes in population distribution and population projection at county level in China. Humanit Soc Sci Commun 11(1):1–13.                    https://doi.org/10.1057/s41599-024-02784-1                                    ")). As the ageing process accelerates, the proportion of sick, disabled and semi-disabled elderly people in China is also continuously increasing, posing new challenges to both the elderly care service system and the health care system. A study by Peking University revealed that by 2030, the population of elderly individuals with disabilities in China is expected to exceed 77 million, and these individuals are expected to experience a disability period of up to 7.44 years (Luo et al. [2021](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR42 "Luo YN, Su BB, Zheng XY (2021) Trends and challenges for population and health during population aging—China, 2015–2050. China CDC Wkly 3(28):593–598.                    https://doi.org/10.46234/ccdcw2021.158                                    ")). As the number of disabled elderly individuals in China grows, so does the demand for professional nursing and medical services. The traditional elderly care model mainly provides basic daily care and lacks necessary medical support, which makes it difficult for elderly individuals to receive timely health interventions in elderly care institutions. Concurrently, the hospitals’ inpatient services have also failed to meet the special care needs of elderly individuals, especially in the care of chronic disease patients. Therefore, how to reform the elderly care system and achieve an effective integration of medical care and daily life assistance has become the key to ensuring that the health requirements of elderly individuals are met. At the same time, the Outline of the Healthy China 2030 Plan ( [2016](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR13 "CPC Central Committee, State Council (2016) Outline of Healthy China 2030.                    https://www.gov.cn/                                    ")) set the goals of continuously improving people’s health, effectively controlling major health risk factors, and significantly improving health service capabilities by 2030. In this context, the combination of medical and elderly care, as an important measure to optimize the health of elderly individuals and the supply of pension services, is highly important for achieving these goals.

The concept of the combination of medical and nursing care first appeared in the late 20th century. It refers to the combination of elderly care services and medical services to meet the health and nursing needs of elderly individuals (Hlebec et al. [2016](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR24 "Hlebec V, Srakar A, Majcen B (2016) Care for the elderly in Slovenia: a combination of informal and formal care. Rev Za Soc Polit 23:159–179.                    https://doi.org/10.3935/rsp.v23i2.1317                                    "); Du et al. [2021](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR19 "Du N, Wu P, Yuan M, Li Z (2021) Performance evaluation of combining with medical and old-age care in pension institutions of China: a two-stage data envelopment analysis. Risk Manag Health Policy 14:4211–4222.                    https://doi.org/10.2147/RMHP.S332880                                    ")).This concept is proposed to increase service efficiency and quality by combining medical and elderly care resources, thereby ensuring that elderly individuals have access to more convenient and efficient services in both areas (Wenger et al. [2003](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR65 "Wenger NS, Solomon DH, Roth CP et al. (2003) The quality of medical care provided to vulnerable community-dwelling older patients. Ann Intern Med 139(9):740–747.                    https://doi.org/10.7326/0003-4819-139-9-200311040-00008                                    "); Karlsson et al. [2008](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR31 "Karlsson S, Edberg A-K, Westergren A, Hallberg IR (2008) Functional ability and health complaints among older people with a combination of public and informal care vs. public care only. Scand J Caring Sci 22(1):136–148.                    https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1471-6712.2007.00549.x                                    ")). As an innovative elderly care model for coping with population ageing, the combination of medical and elderly care is gradually gaining widespread attention in countries around the world. Through years of exploration and practice, Japan has established a distinctive medical and nursing care system centered on the long-term care insurance system, and it has achieved the organic integration of medical care, nursing, and daily life support and has built a community-based integrated “regional comprehensive care service system”. More importantly, the system is centered on elderly individuals, integrating services such as medical care, preventive health care, nursing, and daily living assistance, forming a unique Japan-based model of combined medical and elderly care (Song and Tang [2019](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR58 "Song PP, Tang W (2019) The community-based integrated care system in Japan: Health care and nursing care challenges posed by super-aged society. Biosci Trends 13(3):279–281.                    https://doi.org/10.5582/bst.2019.01173                                    ")). Since the 1940s, Germany has developed forest therapy, with a focus on forest landscapes, oxygen-rich air, organic food, and wellness culture as core elements. It offers services such as forest vacations, rehabilitation, and wellness activities, forming a complete forest therapy service system and integrating it into the national health care system and effectively reducing national health care expenditures (Zhang and Ye [2022](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR81 "Zhang ZY, Ye B (2022) Forest therapy in Germany, Japan, and China: Proposal, development status, and future prospects. Forests 13(8):1289.                    https://doi.org/10.3390/f13081289                                    ")). The United States supports community elderly care services through the Medicare system, forming a home-based medical and elderly care model centered on family doctors. At the same time, it has developed a diversified long-term care service system, including professional nursing institutions and assisted living facilities, to meet the medical and elderly care requirements of elderly individuals (Brugemann and Manovskii [2010](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR6 "Brugemann B, Manovskii I (2010) Fragility: a quantitative analysis of the US health insurance system. Dissertation, Yale University")). In contrast, China’s combination of medical and elderly care industries is in a period of rapid development. The government places high importance on this sector and has introduced policies such as the “Guiding Opinions on Further Promoting the Development of Integrated Medical and Elderly Care Services” ( [2022](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR43 "National Health Commission (2022) Guiding opinions on further promoting the development of medical and elderly care integration.                    https://www.gov.cn/                                    ")), aimed at combining medical and health services with elderly care services to optimize the supply of elderly health and care services. These policies particularly emphasize the development of home-based and community-based integrated medical and elderly care services; they encourage institutions to deeply engage in a combination of medical and elderly care services. At the same time, China’s “National Civilized City” initiative has provided a valuable exploration of the optimization of social services (Chen et al. [2022](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR10 "Chen J, Yi C, Wang Y, Bi T (2022) Do honored cities achieve a sustainable development? A quasi-natural experimental study based on “national civilized city” campaign in China. Sustainability 14:16872.                    https://doi.org/10.3390/su142416872                                    ")). This initiative not only enhances the economic and environmental sustainability of cities but also provides a solid social foundation for integrating medical care and elderly support (Yang et al. [2025](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR75 "Yang WX, Yang YP, Chen ZL, Gu YX (2025) Systemic impacts of national civilized cities on sustainable development: A quasi-experimental analysis of economic and environmental outcomes in China. Systems 13(1):23.                    https://doi.org/10.3390/systems13010023                                    ")). Although the combination of medical and elderly care has achieved some results with the strong promotion of various departments, there are still some problems and challenges. These include insufficient policy support, underdeveloped medical professional team construction, uneven service quality, the mismatch between service supply and demand, and the low coverage of medical insurance (Dieng-Kuntz et al. [2006](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR16 "Dieng-Kuntz R, Minier D, Růžička M et al. (2006) Building and using a medical ontology for knowledge management and cooperative work in a health care network. Comput Biol Med 36:871–892.                    https://doi.org/10.1016/j.compbiomed.2005.04.015                                    ")). These issues have led to an urgent need to improve the development mechanism and environment of the combination of medical and elderly care services.

Research on the CMECP is crucial for promoting the continuous optimization and high-quality development of the combination of medical and elderly care services. Such research can identify the characteristics of policy tools in the policy-making process and make appropriate policy recommendations (Oliver et al. [2014](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR44 "Oliver K, Lorenc T, Innvær S (2014) New directions in evidence-based policy research: a critical analysis of the literature. Health Res Policy Syst 12:1–11.                    https://doi.org/10.1186/1478-4505-12-34                                    ")) to improve the combined medical and elderly care service system. In recent years, policy research on the combination of medical and elderly care has received increasing attention globally, with the focus on this research continuously increasing (Kolade et al. [2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR32 "Kolade OR, Porat-Dahlerbruch J, Makhmutov R et al. Health Res Policy Syst 22(1):26.                    https://doi.org/10.1186/s12961-024-01107-9                                    ")). In China, this research mainly focuses on policy theory, practical models, service model evaluation, and medical professional cultivation (Wang et al. [2023](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR64 "Wang QW, Ma Y, Chen J (2023) Elderly care policy in China: its evolutionary path and implications on governance. Innov Aging 7:1026.                    https://doi.org/10.1093/geroni/igad104.3298                                    ")). However, existing research has focused mostly on the level of current situation analysis and policy recommendations, and quantitative and systematic analysis of the combination of medical and elderly care policy texts is lacking (OuYang [2023](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR45 "OuYang Z (2023) A review of literature on the integration of medical care and elderly care in China based on CiteSpace. Aging Res 10:1790.                    https://doi.org/10.12677/AR.2023.104240                                    ")). At the same time, critical areas such as policy support and service capacity enhancement face challenges (Feng et al. [2020](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR21 "Feng ZL, Glinskaya E, Chen HT et al. (2020) Long-term care system for older adults in China: policy landscape, challenges, and future prospects. Lancet 396:1362–1372.                    https://doi.org/10.1016/S0140-6736(20)32136-X                                    ")). Therefore, determining how to design reasonable and effective policies and promote these policies is crucial to improve health care services for elderly individuals.

The PMC index model, proposed by Ruiz Estrada in 2010 as a method for quantitatively evaluating policy texts (Estrada MAR [2010](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR20 "Estrada MAR (2010) The policy modeling research consistency index (PMC-index). [2022](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR63 "Wang NN, Wang WB, Song T et al. [2022](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR71 "Yang CR, Yin SC, Cui D et al. (2022) Quantitative evaluation of traditional Chinese medicine development policy: a PMC index model approach. Front Public Health 10:1041528.                    https://doi.org/10.3389/fpubh.2022.1041528                                    ")). This model not only has excellent performance in evaluations but also possesses high applicability, showing significant advantages in the quantitative analysis of the CMECP. Moreover, in research on the regional competitiveness of China’s cross-border e-commerce (Yang et al. [2023](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR74 "Yang LF, Liu JH, Yang WX (2023) Impacts of the sustainable development of cross-border e-commerce pilot zones on regional economic growth. Sustainability 16(3):1007.                    https://doi.org/10.3390/su16031007                                    ")), similar quantitative analysis methods have been employed, highlighting the importance of policy evaluation in promoting regional economic development. Based on above considerations, this research uses text mining and the PMC index model to conduct a systematic and comprehensive study of the CMECP (Ball et al. [2011](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR3 "Ball S, Hoskins Kate, Maguire Meg, Braun A (2011) Disciplinary texts: a policy analysis of national and local behaviour policies. Crit Stud Educ 52:1–14.                    https://doi.org/10.1080/17508487.2011.536509                                    ")). First, this study collects the CMECP released by the national government and mines policy texts using the ROST CM6 tool. This step extracts keywords and constructs a social network graph to analyze the relationships between the keywords. Simultaneously, this study introduces the LDA topic modeling method and examines the dynamic evolution of policy themes using Sankey diagrams. Second, this study analyses the development status, trends, and focal points of the policies by building a quantitative evaluation system for the CMECP based on the PMC index model. Following this foregoing procedure, this study helps identify the composition, characteristics, and shortcomings of the current CMECP, providing a reference for improving the policy system. Through the process above and in conjunction with existing research, this study aims to systematically analyze the policy texts on the combination of medical and elderly care and identify their components, characteristics, and shortcomings. Specifically, our study aims to reveal the innovative methods and target groups of combined medical and elderly care, while simultaneously highlighting existing gaps in service coverage and the lack of evaluation processes. On this basis, recommendations are proposed to optimize the policy system for the combination of medical and elderly care in conjunction with the analysis results. These measures aim to effectively address the needs of elderly individuals and thus promote the healthy development of the combined medical and elderly care industry.

## Methodology

### Data sources

The objective of this study is to analyze in depth the development context and implementation effectiveness of China’s CMECP, to address the challenges posed by an ageing population. In China, the concept of “combined medical and elderly care” was officially proposed in 2013. Afterwards, various central ministries and commissions gradually introduced policies to support the combination of medical care and elderly services. This development has primarily progressed through three distinct stages: top-level design (2013–2015), comprehensive planning (2016–2017), and detailed implementation (2018 to the present) (Da and Jia [2022](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR14 "Da J, Jia H (2022) The dynamics of policy change and optimisation path of healthcare integration in China based on the perspective of multi-source flow theory. Chin Hosp 26(12):28–30.                    https://doi.org/10.19660/j.issn.1671-0592.2022.12.08                                    ")). Therefore, the time frame of this study is from September 2013 to October 2024. During the policy search, this study employs keywords such as “medical and elderly care” and “medical and health services alongside elderly care services” to gather notifications, plans, opinions, and other policies closely related to combined medical and elderly care at the national level. The data sources include the Peking University Law Database as well as the official websites of relevant authorities such as the State Council and the National Health Commission. To ensure that the sample is representative, precise, and valid, the following criteria are followed in the sample collection and screening process. First, we focus on the representativeness of the sample in the policy data, prioritizing policy documents issued by national government agencies, which are mainly policies formulated by central government departments and their directly affiliated agencies, and excluding the policies of local governments or related agencies. Second, samples lacking substantial content on the combination of medical and elderly care, those unrelated to the research topic, or those with low research value are excluded. Through these criteria, 34 eligible policy documents were ultimately collected. Notably, this study selects national-level policies based on two considerations. First, national policies have national guiding significance, covering the top-level design, comprehensive planning, and detailed implementation stages of the integration of medical care and elderly care. Hence, they can systematically reflect the overall policy ideas of the country. Second, local policies have analytical limitations. There are significant regional differences in the implementation of the integration of medical care and elderly care in China (economic development, population structure, uneven distribution of resources), leading to excessive disparities in local policy goals and paths. Additionally, the large number and complex hierarchy of policies make it challenging to incorporate them into a unified analytical framework, which could affect the scientific validity of the conclusions.

### Methodology design

To explore the policy of combining medical care and elderly care, this research integrates text mining and policy evaluation as two research methods. First, this research uses the ROST CM6 tool to carry out text mining and to identify the core issues and trends of policies by analyzing the frequency of keywords in policy texts. This step helps reveal the main concerns and directions in the policy texts. Next, to further assess the implementation effects of these policies and their social impact, this study uses the PMC index model as the evaluation tool. The PMC index model is used to conduct a quantitative analysis of a policy’s implementation efficiency, effectiveness, and feedback by integrating a series of evaluation criteria, providing a multidimensional and systematic policy evaluation system. Through the combination of this method and model, this research seeks to provide a thorough analysis and assessment of the CMECP.

## Measures

### Policy text mining

To deeply analyze the CMECP and reveal its core issues and trends, this study adopts ROST CM6 software for text mining and analysis. ROST CM6 is a social computing platform developed by a team of professors at Wuhan University in China. It integrates text analysis functions, including word segmentation, high-frequency words analysis, cluster analysis, etc. (Wang [2022](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR61 "Wang HC (2022) Using ROST CM 6.0 to analyse the use of Chinese and English for psychology identity construction among Chinese university students. Atlantis Press, pp 778–785")). Through advanced text processing technology, this tool extracts keywords from policy texts and constructs a social network graph (Sun and Ding [2023](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR59 "Sun XM, Ding X (2023) Text analysis and inter-provincial comparison of foreign expert service policies in Shandong province. Chin Pers Sci 50(3):35")), and then analyses the semantic relationship between keywords. This step can help mine key information such as policy focus, focus, and implementation path, and provide a basis for policy optimization.

### High-frequency keywords analysis

To gain an in-depth understanding of the core content and development trends of the CMECP, this research carries out a detailed analysis of 34 national level CMECP texts using the ROST CM6 tool. Before the text analysis, the research first eliminated functional words and irrelevant words to ensure that the analysis focused on keywords directly related to the policy theme, to improve the accuracy and effectiveness of the analysis. Subsequently, high-frequency keywords are subsequently extracted, and a word cloud is formed, as shown in Fig. [1](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#Fig1). To gain a more comprehensive understanding of the high-frequency keywords in this field, this study further selects the top 20 high-frequency terms, such as “medical institutions” “services” “elderly care” and “health” (see Table [1](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#Tab1)). These terms intuitively reflect the main focus and critical areas of the CMECP.

Policies emphasize the important role of medical institutions in improving the quality of life of elderly individuals. The frequent use of the terms “combination” and “elderly care” illustrates policies’ goal of promoting a deep combination of medical care and elderly care resources to satisfy the health and caregiving requirements of elderly individuals. In summary, the distribution of high-frequency keywords highlights the core focus of the CMECP, which is to improve service capabilities, integrate resources, promote interdepartmental cooperation, and ensure service quality. Overall, these policies focus on the needs of elderly individuals and aim to achieve the organic integration of medical services and elderly care services. By analyzing these high-frequency keywords, this study can provide support for the effective implementation of policies and provide a reference for identifying and selecting representative typical policies.

### Social network analysis

The social network analysis method not only reveals the intrinsic structure of policies but also provides new perspectives for policy evaluation and formulation (Chen and Chen [2017](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR11 "Chen Y, Chen J (2017) An analysis of social media research hotspots at home and abroad based on keyword co-occurrence and social network analysis. This method not only visualizes the co-occurrence relationships between words but also reveals the internal connections and hierarchies in the policy text. Therefore, social network analysis can provide a reference for policy evaluation and formulation by revealing the priority of policies and the correlation between policies. The social network is shown in Fig. [2](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#Fig2).

### Topic evolution analysis

Based on high-frequency keyword analysis and co-occurrence network analysis, the content of China’s CMECP focus was initially identified. To further explore the temporal evolutionary characteristics of policy themes and to provide a basis for the subsequent establishment of a scientific PMC evaluation system, based on the LDA topic model, this study combines methods such as similarity calculations to construct topic associations between different time series. This study also uses Sankey diagrams to visually present the evolution of themes between different stages of the CMECP. The LDA topic model is a text mining technique based on unsupervised machine learning algorithms. It presents the topic classification of text in the form of probability distributions (DiMaggio et al. [2013](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR17 "DiMaggio P, Nag M, Blei D (2013) Exploiting affinities between topic modeling and the sociological perspective on culture: application to newspaper coverage of U.S. government arts funding. Poetics 41:570–606.                    https://doi.org/10.1016/j.poetic.2013.08.004                                    ")). This study uses the LDA topic model to extract latent thematic information from CMECP texts and takes the analysis results as one of the bases for constructing the PMC index model evaluation index system.

In order to achieve the evolutionary relationships between themes at different stages of CMECP. This research first builds upon the three-phase framework proposed in existing research (Dai et al. [2022](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR15 "Dai S, Zhang W, Lan L (2022) Quantitative evaluation of China’s ecological protection compensation policy based on PMC index model. Int J Environ Res Public Health 19:10227.                    https://doi.org/10.3390/ijerph191610227                                    ")) (top-level design phase from 2013 to 2015, comprehensive planning phase from 2016 to 2017, and detailed implementation phase from 2018 to the present) and further considers the phased characteristics of policy implementation and major policy adjustment nodes, subdividing the third phase into two sub-phases: the deepening promotion period (2018-2021) and the innovative development period (2022–2024).

Based on the stage division above, the following steps are taken to carry out the thematic mining and analysis of policy texts in this study. First, the policies are segmented by period, and then, the LDA topic model is used to mine the themes of CMECP texts (Schmidt [2008](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR53 "Schmidt M (2008) The Sankey diagram in energy and material flow management. J Ind Ecol 12(2):173–185.                    https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1530-9290.2008.00015.x                                    "); Xu and Xi [2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR68 "Xu L, Xi ML (2024) Quantitative evaluation of policies for combining medical and nursing care based on the LDA–PMC model: a comparative analysis of typical Chinese provinces. Public Organ Rev 24(2):693–713.                    https://doi.org/10.1007/s11115-022-00675-0                                    ")). Next, the Log\_Perplexity method is used to calculate the perplexity values corresponding to different topics, with the minimum perplexity value ultimately being determined as the optimal number of topics. Through this step, the extraction of themes is achieved, and corresponding keywords for each theme are also obtained. Finally, based on the keywords, different topics are manually described to achieve topic extraction.

After completing topic extraction, this study further conducts similarity calculations and topic evolution analysis on the policy texts (Jelodar et al. [2019](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR28 "Jelodar H, Wang YL, Yuan C et al. (2019) Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) and topic modeling: models, applications, a survey. Multimed Tools Appl 78:15169–15211.                    https://doi.org/10.1007/s11042-018-6894-4                                    ")). First, the similarity of different dimensional topics is calculated using the TF-IDF model. Based on the similarity calculation results, the relationships and evolutionary patterns between topics in different periods are identified, and a Sankey diagram is drawn to track the changes in topics over time (Bastani et al. [2019](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR5 "Bastani K, Namavari H, Shaffer J (2019) Latent Dirichlet allocation (LDA) for topic modeling of the CFPB consumer complaints. Expert Syst Appl 127:256–271.                    https://doi.org/10.1016/j.eswa.2019.03.001                                    ")), visually demonstrating the inheritance and transformation of the hot spots in the CMECP.

Ultimately, dynamic visualization is achieved through a Sankey diagram, as shown in Fig. [3](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#Fig3). The width of the nodes represents the topic probability weight, the connecting lines reflect the strength of topic inheritance, and the colors distinguish the development stages. This step aims to visually present the policy focus of different stages of China’s CMECP.

### Quantitative evaluation of policies

To deeply understand the development status and policy orientation of the CMECP in China, this study selects the PMC index model to quantitatively evaluate the CMECP. The PMC index model was proposed by (Estrada MAR 2010) on the basis of the Omnia Mobilis hypothesis. This model states that everything is connected, grounded in the principle of the universality of connections. Therefore, it’s crucial to encompass as many pertinent variables as feasible. The advantage of the PMC index model is that it can accurately assess the internal consistency of policies. In addition, the model can also intuitively display the indicators and the overall indicators of the policy through PMC-Surface, to comprehensively evaluate the advantages and disadvantages of the policy (Zhang and Qie [2017](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR80 "Zhang YA, Qie HT (2017) Quantitative evaluation of the impact of financial policy mix on corporate technological innovation: based on the PMC index model. This initiative is beneficial for integrating information resources and achieving information sharing (Ho [2020](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR25 "Ho A (2020) Are we ready for artificial intelligence health monitoring in elder care? BMC Geriatr 20:358.                    https://doi.org/10.1186/s12877-020-01764-9                                    ")), providing information and technical support for integrated medical and elderly care services. The policy also encourages medical institutions to carry out telemedicine services to elderly care facilities and urges various regions to explore new models of a combination of medical and elderly care services based on the Internet. This could help make elderly care services more accessible and targeted (Ahadzadeh et al. [2015](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR1 "Ahadzadeh AS, Sharif SP, Ong FS, Khong KW (2015) Integrating health belief model and technology acceptance model: an investigation of health-related internet use. J Med Internet Res 17(2):e3564.                    https://doi.org/10.2196/jmir.3564                                    ")). However, since this policy was introduced at the initial stage of the combination of medical and elderly care, it lacks comprehensive policy foundations and therefore does not provide specific guidance on service standards. This result indicates that although the policy has excellent performance in terms of many aspects, there are still areas that require further improvement and development.

Policy P8 ranks second, with a PMC index of 7.48. As a policy from the period of rapid development of the CMECP, it is not only clear in its targets but also quite comprehensive in its incentive measures. It has established comprehensive home medical service standards based on actual conditions. At the same time, it emphasizes that the development of combined medical and elderly care services requires enhancing the community’s capacity for integrated services and optimizing the layout of medical resources. At the same time, this policy strengthens the construction of rehabilitation hospitals, nursing homes, and palliative care institutions through new construction, renovation, and expansion. This approach enhances the sharing of medical and elderly care resources. Notably, the policy proposes leveraging the advantages of traditional Chinese medicine and the combination of Chinese and Western medicine for health maintenance and chronic disease prevention, promoting the integration of traditional Chinese medicine into families, communities, and institutions. These approaches use traditional Chinese medicine’s advantages in prevention, health care, disease treatment, and rehabilitation to improve the health of elderly individuals, particularly in the prevention and management of chronic diseases (Li and Wang [2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR35 "Li X, Wang HH (2024) Issues and management strategies in integrated medical and elderly care services in traditional Chinese medicine. J Tradit Chin Med 32(18):42–43.                    https://doi.org/10.16690/j.cnki.1007-9203.2024.18.040                                    ")). As a policy introduced in the wake of the COVID-19 pandemic, it highlights the importance of taking responsibility for the prevention and control of infectious diseases. In addition, the policy stipulates specific preventive measures that should be taken by health care facilities during a major infectious disease outbreak. This finding indicates that the policy can closely align with the current social situation and promptly respond to public health events. However, the policy has shortcomings in predicting and planning the future development of integrated medical and elderly care services, as it fails to fully consider long-term needs. This finding suggests that policy-makers need to pay more attention to the foresight and sustainability of future policies to adapt to the upcoming changes and challenges.

The PMC index of policy P1 is 7.47, ranking third. It is a policy with medium to long-term planning that actively addresses the challenges of an ageing population. The formulation of this policy is well founded, it has clear objectives, and it aligns with China’s current ageing situation, reflecting the country’s emphasis on forward-looking planning for the development of the elderly care service industry. The content of the policy involves the transformation of the barrier-free environment in the community, the construction of a home-based elderly care service network, and the strengthening of the construction of elderly care institutions. Additionally, the policy emphasizes the development of cultural and recreational services for elderly individuals and home-based network information services to meet the diverse service needs of elderly individuals.

A major innovation of the policy is that it proposes increasing insurance support and including qualified medical institutions in the elderly care institutions in medical insurance management. At the same time, the policy also encourages the revitalization of land resources for the construction of integrated medical and elderly care projects. However, when the policy was implemented, the development of the combination of medical care and maintenance in China was still in the initial stage. As a result, the policy provides guidance only at the macro level and lacks prediction for the specific implementation of the combined service of medical care and elderly care.

### Acceptable policy analysis

This policy emphasizes the role of traditional Chinese medicine in the combination of medical and elderly care services, increasing the diversity of services. Policy P7 also highlights the promotion of information technology applications, which helps improve service efficiency and quality. ## Discussion

To thoroughly assess the practical impact and execution of the CMECP, this article conducts an in-depth discussion and analysis of the policy from three critical dimensions: policy timeliness, policy tools, and policy content. In addition, this study also conducts a comparative analysis of its own research results with existing studies on the CMECP. This comparative analysis not only verifies the reliability of this study but also further improves the research system, thereby laying a foundation for proposing more targeted recommendations in the future.

From the perspective of policy timeliness, only one of the 12 policy samples is predictive. Additionally, only policies P3, P6, and P9 involve short-term or medium-term plans, and no policies involve long-term plans. The most concave shape of the primary indicator {X}\_{2} in the radar chart also indicates that the policy timeliness indicator does not perform well. This result indicates that the immediacy of the CMECP has obvious advantages, but there are also some shortcomings. The benefit of immediacy lies in the ability to quickly respond to current social needs and challenges, ensuring that policies swiftly adapt to the current demands of medical and elderly care services and providing prompt services and support. However, the disadvantage of policies lacking medium-term and long-term planning is that they may result in insufficient policy continuity and sustainability. Policies without long-term plans may lead to uneven resource allocation and make it difficult to address future challenges such as the intensification of population ageing and the increase in medical demands (Sharp et al. [2018](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR54 "Sharp D, Lorenc A, Little P et al. (2018) Complementary medicine and the NHS: experiences of integration with UK primary care. Eur J Integr Med 24:8–16.                    https://doi.org/10.1016/j.eujim.2018.10.009                                    ")). For example, the CMECP in the United Kingdom emphasizes achieving seamless integration between the two services, but its starting point is first to clearly distinguish between the two services and implement tiered diagnosis and treatment (Zhou et al. [2015](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR83 "Zhou SH, Xie M, Kwan M-P (2015) Ageing in place and ageing with migration in the transitional context of urban China: a case study of ageing communities in Guangzhou. Habitat Int 49:177–186.                    https://doi.org/10.1016/j.habitatint.2015.05.022                                    ")). This distinction is beneficial for short-term medical services, but without mid-term and long-term planning, it may lead to fragmentation of services, making sustainable optimization and advancement difficult. Through analysis, it is evident that the current CMECP is primarily an immediate policy. This allows policies to respond quickly to current elderly care service needs, but the lack of medium-term and long-term planning affects the long-term effectiveness and sustainability of the policies.

To address the limitations of policies, policy-makers can adopt the following strategies. First, they can clarify the positioning of community medical and elderly care services within the “Healthy China 2030” strategy and advance them with short-term, medium-term, and long-term goals to meet the diverse needs of the increasing ageing population. On this basis, the state can establish a dynamic evaluation and adjustment mechanism, assess policy effectiveness every 3–5 years, and adjust policy goals, resource allocation, and service models. In addition, social capital and innovative models can be introduced to carry out mutual assistance elderly care services, further enriching the practical path of the “Healthy China 2030” strategy (Tan et al. [2019](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR60 "Tan XD, Zhang YN, Shao HY (2019) Healthy China 2030, a breakthrough for improving health. Glob Health Promot 26(4):96–99.                    https://doi.org/10.1177/1757975917743533                                    ")). Through these strategies, the shortcomings of policies lacking medium- and long-term planning can be addressed, responding to the challenges of future population ageing and the growth in health care demand.

From the policy tool perspective, the majority of the 12 policy samples use either mandatory or hybrid policy instruments, whereas only three policies use voluntary policy instruments, which is a rather uneven distribution. This finding shows that the current CMECP in China mainly relies on mandatory and hybrid tools and does not often use voluntary policy tools. This preference may stem from the fact that mandatory and mixed policy tools can provide more direct control and more predictable outcomes, which are crucial for ensuring the achievement of policy targets. However, this imbalanced choice of tools may limit the flexibility and innovation of policies. Research by Kane et al. ( [2007](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR30 "Kane RL, Roebuck-Spencer T, Short P et al. Arch Clin Neuropsychol 22:S115–S126.                    https://doi.org/10.1016/j.acn.2006.10.006                                    ")) in the United States indicates that the adoption of voluntary policy tools can improve the acceptance of policy services in specific situations. These tools encourage service providers and consumers to participate in the practice of combined medical and elderly services by offering incentives, thereby increasing the attractiveness of and participation in the policy. Based on the analysis above, Chinese policy-makers can consider how to balance and optimize the use of various policy tools to achieve a harmonious CMECP, thereby enhancing the effectiveness and target alignment of policies.

## Conclusion and recommendations

Over the past few years, with the rapid development of China’s combination of medical and elderly care industry, a series of relevant policies have emerged to provide effective guidance for the standardization and overall development of this industry. This study comprehensively evaluated the prevailing progress and trajectory of the CMECP, identified the policy focus, composition, and shortcomings, and subsequently provided strategic recommendations for optimizing the policy. Specifically, this study takes the CMECP issued by the central government from 2013 to 2024 as the research object, and carries out text mining by extracting keywords and constructing social network maps. Simultaneously, based on existing research, an evaluation system for the CMECP was developed utilizing the PMC index model, and a thorough analysis of the assessment outcomes was conducted.

The findings of this research can be summarized as follows. (1) From a general policy perspective, China’s CMECP is currently in a favorable period, as evidenced by the increasing number of policy issuances, particularly those jointly issued by multiple departments. Additionally, the formats of policy texts have become more diverse, and their regulatory nature has been continuously strengthened, fully reflecting the Chinese government’s high regard for the evolution of the combination of medical and elderly care. However, the actual implementation effects of these policies still need further evaluation. (2) In terms of policy content, China’s CMECP has demonstrated excellent comprehensiveness in certain critical areas. Comprehensiveness is specifically reflected in promoting information technology and promoting the integration of traditional Chinese and Western medicine. These aspects highlight the significant foresight and innovation of the CMECP. However, the analysis also revealed that policy support in certain key areas, such as service standardization, professional talent development, and resource optimization, still needs to be strengthened. This deficiency aligns with the current stage of development of China’s integrated medical and elderly care industry. As the industry continues to mature, the policy focus and investment efforts in these areas are expected to gradually increase. (3) From the aspects of policy evaluation, 3 of the 12 policies are rated as “good”, 9 are rated as “acceptable”, and there are no “low” policies. The findings indicate that China’s CMECP has received a high degree of acceptance overall. Nevertheless, these policies still have some shortcomings in terms of incentives, long-term planning, and specific operations, and the choice and application of policy tools are relatively homogeneous. These deficiencies need to be addressed, and future policy formulation and implementation need to be optimized to further improve policy effectiveness. For example, future policy optimization could focus on the diversity of tool selection to enhance the adaptability and effectiveness of the policies.

From the perspective of integrating medical resources and elderly care resources, this study aims to promote the sustainable development of combined medical and elderly care services. Based on the analysis results, this research puts forward the following suggestions:

1. (1)
Policy-makers should balance short-, medium-, and long-term goals; adopt various policy tools; integrate information technology with the innovative application of traditional Chinese medicine; and improve service quality and efficiency. In the short term (1-2 years), led by the National Health Commission, in collaboration with the Ministry of Civil Affairs, a special policy promotion group for the integration of medical care and elderly care will be established. This group will be responsible for coordinating and overseeing the formulation and implementation of relevant policies. The group needs to develop a detailed plan for the integration of medical care and elderly care services, clarify the responsibilities of each department, and ensure the smooth advancement of the policies. In the medium term (3-5 years), the National Health Commission is responsible for promoting the application of information technology in integrated medical and elderly care services, establishing a unified information management platform for integrated medical and elderly care, and achieving interconnectivity between medical and elderly care data. The Ministry of Civil Affairs is responsible for integrating elderly care resources, optimizing the layout and functions of elderly care institutions, and improving the supply capacity of elderly care services. At the same time, the two departments need to jointly formulate evaluation standards and quality supervision mechanisms for integrated medical and elderly care services, and regularly assess the quality of services and the effectiveness of resource integration. In the long term (5 years or more), the National Health Commission and the Ministry of Civil Affairs should continuously deepen the innovation of the integrated medical and elderly care service model, explore the establishment of a multi-level integrated medical and elderly care service system, and meet the diverse needs of different elderly groups. Social capital should be encouraged to participate in the construction and operation of integrated medical and elderly care services, forming a multi-faceted development pattern led by the government and involving social participation.

2. (2)
Efforts to cultivate medical professionals should be increased by implementing a series of incentive policies, such as improving salaries and benefits, providing career development opportunities, and establishing incentive mechanisms to attract and retain medical professionals (Yang [2020](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR72 "Yang L (2020) Construction of integrated health care and elderly care talent training system for health care and elderly care major in higher vocational education under combination of medical treatment and endowment. In: 2020 International Symposium on Advances in Informatics, Electronics and Education (ISAIEE). pp 231–236")). As specific measures, the National Health Commission should coordinate relevant departments to formulate salary guidelines for integrated medical and elderly care service positions, ensuring that the salary levels of medical professionals match the intensity of their work and their professional value. The Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security can be responsible for supervising and implementing these measures to ensure the rationality and stability of the salary and benefits.

3. (3)
Government agencies should strengthen the promotion of and training on the concept of integrated medical and nursing services, increase the awareness and interest of medical staff, and thereby promote the deep integration of resources (Lin [2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR38 "Lin X (2024) Research on the operation mechanism of community home-based elderly care services under the perspective of embedded theory: a case study based on practices in Shanghai. Health Vocat Educ 42(20):129–134.                    https://link.cnki.net/doi/10.20037/j.issn.1671-1246.2024.20.36                                    ")). The National Health Commission and the Ministry of Civil Affairs jointly formulated a publicity plan for the concept of integrated medical and nursing services. This plan aims to widely promote the advantages and importance of integrated medical and nursing services through various channels (such as television, newspapers, the internet, and community bulletin boards), thereby increasing public awareness and acceptance. At the same time, special promotional activities targeting medical staff will be conducted. Through lectures and seminars, the aim is to enhance medical staff’s understanding and recognition of the concept of integrated medical and elderly care service.

4. (4)
Policy-makers should place greater emphasis on developing integrated medical and elderly care services in families and communities. Specific measures may include encouraging medical institutions to provide home visit services and supporting the construction of community-integrated medical and elderly care services (Pani-Harreman et al. [2022](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR47 "Pani-Harreman KE, van Duren JMA, Kempen GIJM, Bours GJJW (2022) The conceptualisation of vital communities related to ageing in place: a scoping review. Through these comprehensive measures, it is possible to ensure that resources combining medical and elderly care services are reasonably allocated, thereby addressing the growing medical and elderly care needs of the elderly population (Horner and Boldy [2008](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05746-3#ref-CR26 "Horner B, Boldy DP (2008) The benefit and burden of “ageing-in-place” in an aged care community. At the same time, through policies such as government procurement of services and tax incentives, social forces are guided to participate in the construction and operation of community-integrated medical and elderly care services, thereby improving the supply capacity and quality of these services.


Overall, the CMECP has achieved certain results in promoting the deep integration of medical services and elderly care services. However, it still needs to be optimized in terms of policy continuity, comprehensiveness, and operability to better meet the medical and elderly care needs of elderly individuals.

The unique advantages of this study are as follows. First, from the research perspective, this study overcomes the limitations of existing studies, which often focus on descriptive accounts and policy recommendations. By innovatively introducing the PMC index model for systematic quantitative analysis of CMECP policy texts, our study fills the gap in quantitative research in this field, providing a new theoretical analysis framework for the development of the CMECP in China. At the same time, this study innovatively conducts a dynamic topic evolution of CMECP policies based on the LDA topic model and visualizes it via a Sankey diagram. This multi-method integrated research design not only enhances the uniqueness of this study but also improves the comprehensiveness and reference value of the analysis. The research findings provide new theoretical perspectives and research ideas for the future development of China’s CMECP.

However, this study also has some limitations. First, this study rigorously and selectively screened policy samples, focusing on policies published at the national level and eliminating those published at the local level. Although national-level policies have macro-guiding and representative functions, there are significant differences in economic development levels and industrial structures across various regions in China. Local policies are often more flexible and targeted. Ignoring local policies may limit the generalizability of the research conclusions, making it impossible to fully reflect the implementation characteristics and effects of policies in different regions. In the future, the scope of local policy data can be expanded to conduct a series of discussions on the CMECP from the national level to the local level. Moreover, this study is primarily based on content analysis of policy texts and lacks feedback from strategy recipients. Although this method can reveal the orientation, aims, and tool design of policies, it does not include a study of the policies’ actual execution or input from recipients. This can result in a mismatch between the idealized language in policy texts and the actual implementation outcomes. For example, certain policies may stress environmental protection in the text, but in fact, environmental protection may be undermined owing to economic constraints. Therefore, future research could integrate qualitative methods such as interviews and surveys to explore the implementation experiences and actual effects of the CMECP, thereby providing a more comprehensive understanding of these policies.
